GPA Guide for Nigeria: The 5-Point CGPA System Explained

Nigerian universities calculate CGPA on a 5-point scale, not the 4.0 scale used in the US. Here's exactly how it works, the degree classification cutoffs, and a full worked example using credit units.

Worked example with credit units

Nigerian courses are measured in "credit units" rather than credit hours, but the math works identically — grade point multiplied by credit units gives quality points, and total quality points divided by total credit units gives CGPA.

How it compares to the US 4.0 scale

The mechanics are identical — both systems divide total quality points by total credits — but the ceiling and score bands differ. A Nigerian CGPA of 4.50 (First Class) is not simply "4.50 out of 4.0 US GPA"; it roughly corresponds to a US GPA near the top of the scale, since both represent the highest attainable classification within their own system. When applying internationally, use your target university's official conversion table rather than a straight proportional calculation.

Tips for Nigerian students

  • Track CGPA every session, not just at graduation — many postgraduate programs and NYSC-adjacent opportunities reference cumulative standing throughout your studies.
  • Know your carry-over policy. Some institutions average a resit grade with the original; others replace it entirely — this materially changes how a resit affects CGPA.
  • Elective credit units still count. A low grade in a "less important" 1-unit elective has less drag on CGPA than the same grade in a 4-unit core course, so prioritize study time accordingly.

FAQ

What CGPA is needed for a First Class in Nigeria?

Most Nigerian universities require a CGPA of 4.50 to 5.00 on the 5-point scale, though exact cutoffs are set by each institution's senate.

Do all Nigerian universities use the 5-point scale?

It's the most common approach, but a small number of institutions use a 4-point or 7-point scale instead — confirm with your own school's academic regulations.

How is Nigerian CGPA different from US GPA?

Both use the same underlying quality-points-divided-by-credits method, but the Nigerian scale tops out at 5.0 rather than 4.0, and uses credit units rather than credit hours.
