Conversion formulas by scale

  • 4.0 scale: Percentage ≈ GPA × 25
  • 5.0 scale (Nigeria): Percentage ≈ CGPA × 20
  • 7.0 scale (Australia): Percentage ≈ (GPA ÷ 7) × 100
  • 10.0 scale (India, CBSE): Percentage ≈ CGPA × 9.5

These are the most widely cited approximations for each system, but they are still approximations — see why exactness isn't possible below.

Why this is an estimate, not exact

GPA-to-percentage conversion is inherently approximate because the two systems don't measure the same thing. A percentage reflects raw marks; a GPA reflects a bucketed letter grade that already threw away some precision. Two students with 89% and 93% might both land on an A- to A in a given school's grade bands, which converts back to the same GPA even though the raw marks differed by four points. Always use your institution's official conversion table for anything that affects an actual application or transcript.

FAQ

Is GPA to percentage conversion exact?

No — it's an approximation. Different institutions use different formulas, and grading systems aren't perfectly linear, so treat the result as an estimate rather than an official figure.

Why does India use a 9.5 multiplier instead of 10?

CBSE and many affiliated institutions found that raw marks historically averaged roughly 95% of ten times the CGPA, so 9.5 became the standard approximation rather than a straightforward times-ten calculation.
