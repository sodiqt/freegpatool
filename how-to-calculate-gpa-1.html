The GPA formula

At its core, GPA is a weighted average:

GPA = Total Quality Points ÷ Total Credit Hours

"Quality points" are just a grade's point value multiplied by how many credits that course is worth. Credit weighting is the part most people get wrong when estimating by hand — a 4-credit class swings your average roughly twice as much as a 2-credit class with the same grade.

4 steps to calculate GPA by hand

  1. List every course, its letter grade, and its credit hours for the term you're calculating.
  2. Convert each letter grade to points using your school's scale (the standard US 4.0 scale is below).
  3. Multiply grade points by credit hours for each course to get that course's quality points.
  4. Add all quality points together, add all credit hours together, then divide the first sum by the second.

Full worked example

Here's a real semester, calculated in full:

World HistoryB+3.339.9
Chemistry LabB3.026.0
Intro to EconomicsA4.0312.0

42.7 ÷ 12 = 3.56 GPA. Notice that Calculus II, worth 4 credits, contributed more to the final number than Chemistry Lab's 2 credits — even though the letter grades were only one step apart.

This is the most common US scale, but always confirm against your own institution's official grading policy — some schools don't use plus/minus grades at all, or cap A+ below 4.0.

Pass/fail, withdrawals, and retakes

  • Pass/fail courses usually carry no grade points and are excluded from the GPA formula entirely, even though the credits may still count toward graduation.
  • Withdrawals (W) typically don't affect GPA, but a late withdrawal deadline varies by school — check before assuming a W is "free."
  • Retaken courses either use grade replacement (only the newer grade counts) or averaging (both grades count), depending entirely on your institution's policy.

FAQ

Does GPA calculation include failed classes?

Yes, in most systems an F counts as 0.0 points and its credits are still included in the total, which lowers your GPA rather than simply being ignored.

Do pass/fail classes count toward GPA?

Usually not — they typically carry no grade points and are excluded from the calculation, although the credit hours may still count toward your degree requirements.

How many decimal places should I use?

Most US transcripts round to two decimal places, like 3.56, though some schools use one decimal place or keep the unrounded figure internally.
