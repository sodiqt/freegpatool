The CGPA formula

CGPA is a credit-weighted average of every semester's GPA — not a plain average of the numbers themselves. That's exactly why this calculator asks for each semester's credit total, not just the GPA: a semester carrying 18 credits should influence your CGPA more than one carrying 12.

FAQ

What's the difference between this and the GPA calculator?

The GPA calculator works from individual course grades for one term. This tool works from each semester's already-known GPA and credit total, combining several semesters into one cumulative figure.

What if I don't know a past semester's exact GPA?

Check your official transcript or student portal — most institutions list each term's GPA alongside your cumulative figure. An estimated GPA will only give an approximate result.
